WHAT IS BMI?

Body mass index (BMI) is a person’s weight in kilograms divided by the square of height in meters. BMI is an inexpensive and easy screening method for weight category—underweight, healthy weight, overweight, and obesity. BMI appears to be as strongly correlated with various metabolic and disease outcome as are these more direct measures of body fatness.

Formula: weight (kg) / [height (m)]2 With the metric system, the formula for BMI is weight in kilograms divided by height in meters squared. Example: Weight = 68 kg, Height = 165 cm (1.65 m) Calculation: 68 ÷ (1.65)2 = 24.98

For adults 20 years old and older, BMI is interpreted using standard weight status categories and same for men and women of all body types and ages. The standard weight status categories associated with BMI ranges for adults are shown above.

The correlation between the BMI and body fatness is fairly strong, but even if two people have the same BMI, their level of body fatness may differ. In general, At the same BMI, women tend to have more body fat than men, older people, on average, tend to have more body fat than younger adults. At the same BMI, athletes have less body fat than do non-athletes.
